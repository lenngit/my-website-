The Research

Most estate planning firms are losing 30–40% of their leads in intake, and don't know it.

The average elder law firm takes 2 to 6 hours to respond to a new inquiry. A family dealing with a Medicaid deadline is not waiting. They found three firms on Google. The one that responded first got the consultation. The other two never knew the lead existed.

This is not a marketing problem. It is what happens after the lead arrives. Most firms have no structured follow-up sequence, no pipeline visibility, and no way to measure where drop-off is occurring. Our audit shows you exactly where yours is leaking and what it is costing you every month.

The Audit

What we review across 30 checkpoints.

01
First Response Speed and Follow-Up Sequence

We test your firm's actual response time to a new inquiry, during business hours, after hours, and on weekends. We document whether an automated acknowledgment exists, how long before human contact, and whether a structured follow-up sequence runs for leads that don't book immediately.

02
Ad-to-Intake Tracking and Attribution

If you run any paid advertising (Google LSA, PPC, Facebook, or LinkedIn), we identify whether your conversion events are firing, whether leads can be traced back to their source, and whether you have the data to make intelligent spend decisions. We flag every attribution gap.

03
Intake Form and Booking Flow Friction

We audit the path from first contact to confirmed appointment. How many steps does it take? What information is requested before trust is established? Can a prospect book a consultation without calling or emailing your office? Every point of friction is documented and ranked by drop-off impact.
